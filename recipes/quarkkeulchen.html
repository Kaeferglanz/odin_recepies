Recipe for Sächsische Quarkkeulchen

Quarkkäulchen (German: little quark balls) is a Saxon dish made from dough containing about two-thirds mashed potatoes, one-third quark cheese, eggs and flour, and perhaps spiced with cinnamon or dotted with raisins. The dough is fried in butter or clarified butter into small pancakes. These are served hot, usually with sugar, fruits or other sweet side dishes.

  • 500g Potatoes
  • 375g of Low Fat Curd Cheese
  • 150g of Flour
  • 65g of Sugar
  • 2 Eggs
  • 1 Pinch of Salt
  • Raisins (if wanted)
  • Grated Lemon Zest

Preperation: Cook the potatoes the day prior to baking.

  1. Step

    2. Grate the potatoes and mix them with the low fat curd chees, flour, sugar, eggs, grated lemon zest and a pinch of salt. Should the dough be too wet, add some flour or semolina. Finally, add some raisins.

  2. Step

    3. Form a long roll out of the dough and cut it in 1,5cm thick slices.

  3. Step

    4. Fry the slices in hot fat from both side untill they tale on a gold-brown texture.

  4. Step

    5. Last but not least, serve the Quarkkeulchen right out of the pan with apple puree and/or cinnamon and suggar.
